How I keep on improving my Data Privacy in 2020

Data privacy has been a hot subject even before GDPR (25 May 2018) and CCPA (1st January 2020) became officially applicable.

I actually discussed this very subject early 2017, discussing the various open source tools I opted for at the time.

Anyway, 2 years on, let’s make a quick assessment of what was succesful and kept, and what was not and ditched, as well as look into the other tools I am currently using to ensure my data pricacy is in great shape.

  • Search engine: Duckduckgo is (still) my preferred choice. It’s being used on both my desktop and mobile for a number of years.
  • Mail: Same applies to Protonmail, my favourite (open-source secured) email provider.
  • file hosting service: at the time I had pushed spideroak as an alternative to dropbox & co. I actually have removed most of my problems by 1. acquiring my own nas and use it as a server, and 2. use syncthing as my tool of choice for synching my day-to-day files between my mac and my mobile.
  • todo: I ditched asana for todo.txt (todotxtmac + Todo.txt for Android by Aditya Bhaskar) which I get synched via syncthing as mentioned above.
  • video streaming: I still use youtube but via newpipe and freetube
  • 2FA: I ditched google authenticator to use freeotp
  • navigation: I ditched Google maps and waze to use OsmAnd
  • other softwares: I make the most of Framalibre and its alternatives.
